Current recording workflow
- Audio is collected through the microphone as mono 16 kHz PCM WAV.
- The collector uses a lightweight energy-based endpointer to segment one spoken word per saved clip.
- Samples are written under data/raw/<keyword>/<session-id>/sample_XXXX.wav.
- Metadata is appended to data/manifests/samples.jsonl.
- The IDE helper entry points are start_recording.py for collection and show_feature_space.py for visualization.

Current feature extraction pipeline
- Each saved clip is loaded from the manifest and converted into one fixed-length feature vector.
- MFCCs are computed frame-by-frame from the waveform.
- The per-frame MFCC matrix is summarized with:
  - 13 MFCC mean values
  - 13 MFCC standard deviation values
- A pitch contour (f0 contour) is estimated frame-by-frame with an autocorrelation-based method.
- The f0 contour is summarized with:
  - mean voiced f0
  - standard deviation of voiced f0
  - voiced ratio
- The final feature vector size is 29 dimensions total.

Current projection and visualization pipeline
- All feature vectors are stacked into a single matrix.
- Features are centered and standardized dimension-wise.
- PCA is used to project the features into 3 dimensions.
- The current dashboard view includes:
  - exact-label clustering on PC1 vs PC2
  - base-keyword plus speaker plots on PC1 vs PC2, PC1 vs PC3, and PC2 vs PC3
  - label centroids on PC1 vs PC2
  - explained-variance bar chart

Interpretation of the current feature space
- The current representation is mainly a pooled summary of timbre and pitch.
- It captures broad spectral shape through MFCC statistics and some voicing/pitch behavior through f0 summary statistics.
- It does not preserve detailed temporal structure of the spoken word beyond coarse variability.
- Because the map is based on PCA, the 2D and 3D views are useful for inspection but they compress a larger 29D space into a much smaller view.

Known caveats in the current snapshot
- The dataset is imbalanced across labels.
- Clip durations vary substantially, so duration-related effects may influence separation.
- Session effects may influence clustering because recordings come from multiple sessions.
- The current feature summary may separate clips by speaker/session/timing artifacts as much as by word identity.


What's working
- Word-level signal genuinely exists — the 4 keyword centroids are meaningfully separated in PC1/PC2
MFCC + F0 is a reasonable feature set; the variance explained is decent across the first 6 PCs
Speaker identity is also being captured, just not in a controlled way


Core problem
- Within-word speaker variance is comparable in magnitude to between-word variance
- The PCA has no concept of what you care about — it just finds the directions of maximum overall variance, which turns out to be a messy mixture of word and speaker differences. Neither is cleanly isolated.

Symptoms
- Same-word clips from different speakers scatter widely (e.g. dance1–dance5 spread across the entire PC1/PC2 plane)
- PC1 appears to be driven by a combination of both factors rather than either cleanly
- PC2 vs PC3 projection is largely noise, overlap between all classes
- The heatmaps showed signal fading past PC6, meaning useful information is concentrated but buried

Root cause
- Unsupervised PCA is the wrong tool for a problem with two known, structured sources of variation. It will never reliably separate word vs speaker because it has no label information to guide the projection.

Next step
- Replacing or supplement PCA with multi-label LDA using combined word+speaker labels
